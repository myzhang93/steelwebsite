## Heat Treatment Guide for 410 Stainless Steel Pipe

1. **Annealing:** 750–850 °C, furnace cool to 600 °C, then air cool → softest state for machining.  
2. **Hardening:** 950–1050 °C, oil or air quench → full martensite, ~45 HRC.  
3. **Tempering:**  
   - 600 °C → 45 HRC, highest strength, moderate toughness  
   - 750 °C → 38 HRC, optimal impact resistance for valve stems  

All treatments must be performed after welding to restore **410 stainless steel heat treatment pipe** properties.

**Q1:** Is 410 stainless steel pipe magnetic?  
**A1:** Yes, 410 is fully martensitic and ferromagnetic—ideal for magnetic-particle inspection.

**Q2:** Can 410 pipe be hardened after welding?  
**A2:** Absolutely; solution anneal at 950 °C, quench, then temper 600–750 °C to restore hardness.

**A3:** 410S has &lt;0.08 % C for better weldability but cannot be hardened above 22 HRC.
