## What are 201L Stainless Steel Fittings? (UNS S20103)  
**201L stainless steel fittings** are the extra-low-carbon version of grade 201 (C ≤0.03 %), minimizing carbide precipitation during welding and severe cold forming. High manganese (7.5–10 %) and nitrogen (≤0.25 %) maintain a fully austenitic structure, giving **201L stainless steel properties** comparable to 304 for urban corrosion, deep drawing and mirror polishing—yet at a fraction of the nickel cost. Compared with 201, 201L offers superior weld corrosion resistance; against 304/304L, it provides similar strength and formability while costing 30–45 % less.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1:** What is the difference between 201 and 201L stainless steel fittings?  
**A:** 201L has ≤0.03 % carbon vs 0.15 % max in 201, reducing carbide precipitation and improving weld corrosion resistance.

**Q2:** Are 201L stainless steel fittings food-safe or potable water approved?  
**A:** Yes, they meet FDA and GB 4806.9 for non-acidic potable water and food contact where chloride &lt;50 ppm.

**Q3:** Can 201L fittings be used outdoors?  
**A:** Suitable in urban or low-chloride environments; for coastal salt-spray, 316L is recommended.

**Q4:** Is 201L stainless steel magnetic?  
**A:** Essentially non-magnetic in annealed condition; permeability &lt;1.005 even after cold forming.

**Q5:** Why are 201L fittings much cheaper than 304/304L?  
**A:** Reduced nickel raw-material cost and more stable alloy pricing shield buyers from LME nickel volatility.

**Q6:** Is welding filler required for 201L fittings?  
**A:** Not mandatory for many TIG applications; ER308L or ER201L can be used for color match.

**Q7:** Is 201L stronger than regular 201?  
**A:** Yes, similar yield but better ductility and weld corrosion resistance due to lower carbon and higher nitrogen.
